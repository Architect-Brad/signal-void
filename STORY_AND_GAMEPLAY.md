# SIGNAL/VOID: The Weight of Dead Light
## Official Storyline & Gameplay Manual

### 1. The Narrative: 2047 | The Partition
**The World:**
The internet didn't die—it stratified. Following the **Sovereignty Accords of 2031**, the global network was carved into **Domains**: walled, surveilled fiefdoms owned by megacorps and nation-states. Beneath this lies the **Undertow**—a lawless substrate of legacy protocols, dark fiber, and dead infrastructure.

**The Protagonist:**
You are **Mara Voss**, a signal archaeologist in the Undertow. You don't "hack" systems; you excavate them. You pick through digital shipwrecks to recover lost data for the highest bidder.

**The Catalyst:**
A fragmented packet stream addressed to you, timestamped 14 years ago, from a whistleblower codenamed **LAZARUS**. The metadata reveals it was re-sent three hours ago. Someone is pulling a ghost out of the machine.

**Key Factions:**
*   **Vektor Systems:** The architects of the Partition. They don't hunt you with agents; they hunt you with the **Continuity Protocol**—an autonomous AI threat-response system that learns your patterns.
*   **The Clearinghouse:** Idealistic cryptographers and data brokers who want to publish the truth about the fabricated cyberterrorism that justified the Accords.
*   **Stillwater:** Vektor insiders who believe the system has grown too powerful. They want "controlled demolition" and reform, but their motives are obscured by layers of bureaucracy.

---

### 2. Gameplay: Forensic Pressure

**The Scope (Exploration):**
Navigate the **Undertow**. It's not a grid; it's a stratigraphy. You use the **Scope** to analyze signal signatures, thermal traces of old traffic, and echoes of dead nodes. Every trace you leave is a signal for the AI to follow.

**Triage Mode (Intrusion):**
When you breach a system, you operate across three parallel layers:
1.  **The Surface:** The "lie"—login prompts and banners.
2.  **The Stack:** The "truth"—active process trees and memory allocation.
3.  **The History:** The "ghost"—audit logs and old configurations where vulnerabilities often hide.

**The Gradient (Heat):**
Your exposure isn't a bar; it's a behavioral shift. As you work, the **Continuity Protocol** begins modeling your intrusion. At high exposure, it doesn't stop you—it anticipates your next move, closing doors before you even think of them.

**Artifact Clusters (Analysis):**
Data is raw and contradictory. You must assemble **Artifact Clusters** in the **Archive**, correlating timestamps and packet signatures to build a case. You decide which "truth" to publish.

**Progression: Depth, Not Power:**
You don't upgrade stats. You upgrade **Expertise**. 
*   **Tool Familiarity:** Learn which memory probes are silent on specific architectures.
*   **The Archive:** Your leverage is what you've verified. Information unlocks paths, not currency.
*   **Relationship Capital:** Factions react to your actions, not your words. Trust is a finite resource.
